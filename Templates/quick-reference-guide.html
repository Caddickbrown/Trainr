üéØ Priority Management Matrix

When overwhelmed by competing demands, use this framework to decide what to tackle first.

üî¥ DO NOW

Criteria: Deadline today OR blocks someone else

  • Executive meeting prep (meeting today)
  • Data blocking colleague's analysis
  • Final deadline expiring
  • Production-blocking issue

üü† SCHEDULE

Criteria: Important but can wait 1-2 days

  • Strategic analysis due Friday
  • Performance review (due next week)
  • Process improvement work
  • Training preparation

üîµ DELEGATE

Criteria: Someone else can do it equally well

  • Standard supplier requests
  • Template/document sharing
  • Routine data extracts
  • Training session (team can run)

‚ö™ ELIMINATE

Criteria: Low impact if not done

  • Optional meetings/events
  • Nice-to-have reading
  • Low-priority reports
  • Informal catch-ups

üí° Decision-Making Tips:

  • If in doubt, ask: "What happens if this doesn't get done today?"
  • Time-box decisions: Max 5 minutes to prioritize - imperfect action beats perfect planning
  • Check dependencies: Is anyone waiting on you? That's likely DO NOW
  • Consider energy: Match hard tasks to your peak energy time (usually mornings)
üìä Data Analysis 6-Step Workflow

Follow this structured approach to prevent analysis paralysis and deliver actionable insights.

1

What decision needs to be made? Who needs what by when? (5 min)
2
Gather & Validate Data
Time-box to 15 min. Get obvious sources first. (15 min)
3
Explore Data
Look for patterns, outliers, trends. Quick observations. (10-15 min)
4
Deep Dive
Time-box to 20 min! Investigate root causes and impacts. (20 min)
5
Synthesize Insights
Use WHAT ‚Üí SO WHAT ‚Üí NOW WHAT framework. (15 min)
6
Present & Communicate
Executive summary first. Details in appendix. (10 min)

üí° Analysis Tips for ADHD:

  • Always time-box: Set timers for each step to prevent rabbit holes
  • Document as you go: Don't wait until the end to write insights
  • Use templates: Same structure every time = less decision fatigue
  • Present at 70%: Get feedback early rather than perfecting to 100%
